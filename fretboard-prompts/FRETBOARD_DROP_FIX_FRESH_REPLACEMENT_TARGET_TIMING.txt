TASK
Fix Fretboard Drop horizontal-mode replacement targets that sometimes appear already partway across the lane, especially after a wrong answer or delayed resolution.

ROOT-PROBLEM REVIEW
Before implementation, explicitly answer:

“Are we solving the root problem, or just patching the current architecture?”

The root product rule is simple:

- Horizontal mode shows one active pick at a time.
- Every newly created horizontal pick must begin its own lifetime at the moment it is actually created.
- Its visible progress and gameplay expiration must both derive from the same fresh startedAt timestamp and the same durationMs.
- A wrong answer must not replace the active target or silently change its startedAt.
- A replacement target created after a correct answer or miss recovery must begin at progress 0, regardless of how long the previous target existed.

CURRENT OBSERVED BEHAVIOR
- The recent single-clock RAF repair correctly makes the visible pick position derive from performance.now() and getTargetProgress().
- The current pick now reaches the deadline before disappearing.
- However, after a wrong answer and then resolution, a new letter pick can appear much closer to the deadline, giving much less time to answer.
- A similar early-pop symptom existed before the recent RAF repair, when CSS travel and reducer timing were separate.
- Do not revert the single-clock RAF repair.
- The current new-pick symptom strongly suggests that a replacement target is being created with a stale or scheduled startedAt rather than the actual creation time.
